CHALLENGE

1800-Flowers is underperforming in its ability to reach the millennial market. Millennials are one of the largest generations in history and they are moving into their prime spending years. They are forecasted to reshape the economy and force companies to re-examine how they do business for years to come. Online flower startups are rapidly emerging to meet the unique needs of this tech-savvy and socially conscious generation. 1800flowers.com is at risk of losing a valuable opportunity to capture this market.

 

SOLUTION

Millennials put a premium on speed, efficiency, and convenience in their transactions. Improving the navigation and checkout process across platforms is key to attracting and retaining this consumer base. Through a heuristic analysis and card sort, the page navigation was restructured to avoid unnecessary confusion and improve usability. A mega menu was implemented to accommodate a large number of options while revealing lower-level site pages at a glance. Steps to get through checkout were minimized to decrease cart abandonment. The overall aesthetic and tone of the website was slightly shifted to appeal to the millennial generation while not alienating other audiences.



RESEARCH

This project included the following methods and tools: competitive research, quantitative analysis, user interviews, card sort, personas, and journey maps.

KEY INSIGHTS
  • Millennials currently buy more flowers than any other demographic; 66% of buyers are women
  • Competition is increasing as well-funded start-ups actively target the millennial market
  • 1800flowers.com is underperforming in its ability to reach millennial market both in terms of messaging, social media strategy and the overall aesthetic of their website
  • Multiple navigation bars are confusing

FINAL CONSIDERATIONS

Redesigning 1800flowers.com's navigation and checkout flow improves the browsing and purchasing experience for all users, especially an up and coming generation that values speed and efficiency. However, in analyzing the larger ecosystem, several other key factors need to be taken into consideration:

  • A full content audit and roadmap would improve the quality of 1800flowers.com's content and provide a mechanism to deploy consistent messaging across all platforms.
  • 1800flowers.com needs a social media strategy to reach users on Instagram, Facebook and other social platforms. New flower startups are proving to be much more successful at reaching large audiences on social media.
  • Corporate social responsibility is important to the millennial generation, 1800flowers.com should reprioritize where this information is available on their website and how this information is deployed.
  • Finding new and creative ways to reach the millennial market is key. This generation is already influencing bouquet styles, purchasing options, and delivery methods. Flowers are being delivered on bicycles, in burlap sacks and through monthly subscription services.
