CHALLENGE

Nearly half of low-to-moderate income Denver neighborhoods lack access to healthy food. Residents in these areas, knows as ‘food deserts’, often suffer from diabetes, obesity and other chronic health issues as a result of eating high calorie foods with low nutritional value. How might we provide better access to healthy food in these underserved communities?

SOLUTION

Our team created Bello Box, an affordable meal kit delivery service with a community-centered approach. The Bello Box is designed to make it easier for residents to learn about nutrition, cook quick meals, and provide access to the foods they need to live a healthy lifestyle.

Residents are able to choose from over 100 healthy meal kit recipes. Recipes are searchable by budget and dietary restrictions or specific health issues. Through partnerships with local nonprofit organizations, the meal kit can be delivered at an affordable cost or picked up at the nearest grocery store. Nonprofit partnerships offer additional distribution points for pick-up within the community.

RESEARCH

Our team conducted research in Montbello, a food desert in the Denver metro area. We interviewed residents to better understand their current behaviors around food. We met local nonprofit leaders working on food access issues to gain a holistic view of the problem. We also conducted research current statistics on food access and solutions other cities have piloted to solve this problem.


KEY INSIGHTS
  • Residents are more likely to eat food from convenience stores and fast-food chains because this food is most readily available.
  • Food deserts are often located in low income areas. Transportation can be a challenge for these residents. It's not uncommon for residents to take multiple buses to get to a grocery store.
  • Research shows that putting a grocery store in these areas won’t necessarily solve the problem. Education is key when it comes to changing food buying behavior.
  • When diagnosed with a health issue, like diabetes, there is often confusion around what foods can be eaten and what needs to be avoided.

FINAL CONSIDERATIONS

Redesigning 1800flowers.com's navigation and checkout flow improves the browsing and purchasing experience for all users, especially an up and coming generation that values speed and efficiency. However, in analyzing the larger ecosystem, several other key factors need to be taken into consideration:

  • A full content audit and roadmap would improve the quality of 1800flowers.com's content and provide a mechanism to deploy consistent messaging across all platforms.
  • 1800flowers.com needs a social media strategy to reach users on Instagram, Facebook and other social platforms. New flower startups, such as Bouqs, Farmgirl Flowers, and BloomThat, are proving to be much more successful at reaching large audiences on social media.
  • Corporate social responsibility is important to the millennial generation, 1800flowers.com has several CSR initiatives that are currently buried in the website. Reprioritizing and deploying this information should be part of the content strategy.
  • Finding new and creative ways to reach the millennial market is key. This generation is already influencing bouquet styles, purchasing options, and delivery methods. Flowers are being delivered on bicycles, in burlap sacks and through monthly subscription services.
