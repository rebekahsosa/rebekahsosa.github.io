PROJECT INFO

Role: UX Researcher & UI Designer

Tools: Sketch, Craft & InVision

CHALLENGE

Jiffy Lube’s internal company guidelines state that it takes 8 minutes or less “hood up to hood down” to complete a Signature Service oil change. The reality is frequently a time consuming, inconsistent and neutral experience at best. The high-pressure environment can be stressful for employees and lead to costly mistakes. During the research phase of this project my classmate went in for an oil change and ended up getting a new engine due to a small error made by the mechanic. How might we improve the oil change experience for both customers and employees while improving Jiffy Lube’s bottom-line?

 

FIELD RESEARCH

While I’ve taken my car into Jiffy Lube many times, I wanted to make sure there weren’t any gaps in my understanding of the process. I visited multiple Jiffy Lube locations where I was able to to observe and document the process of getting an oil change and conduct informal interviews with customers and staff.


CURRENT PAIN POINTS
  • Cars in cue outside the Jiffy Lube require a service representative to communicate the current wait time. Flow management is an inefficient use of staff time and long wait times often translate to customers leaving the premises.
  • Reviewing car information with customers inside a busy mechanic shop is a liability.
  • The lobby space lacks imagination, especially for parents with children.
  • Proper employee staffing is a challenge due to the unknown ebb and flow of customer intake.
  • Customers feel lack of trust during up-sell

SERVICE BLUEPRINT

This project included the following methods and tools: competitive research, quantitative analysis, user interviews, card sort, personas, and journey maps.

SOLUTION: CONTROL CUSTOMER FLOW

Our team identified multiple opportunities to improve Jiffy Lube’s bottom-line and improve the experience for customers and employees. Managing traffic outside Jiffy Lube locations was one of the first key opportunities we encountered. Having a designated employee checking in with every customer entering the location inhibits employees from working in shop and leads to longer customer wait times. A timer outside the building notifying customers of wait times would significantly reduce the amount of time representatives spend managing the traffic flow. Customers would be able to quickly check availability and make an informed decision about getting their car serviced.

FINAL CONSIDERATIONS

Redesigning 1800flowers.com's navigation and checkout flow improves the browsing and purchasing experience for all users, especially an up and coming generation that values speed and efficiency. However, in analyzing the larger ecosystem, several other key factors need to be taken into consideration:

  • A full content audit and roadmap would improve the quality of 1800flowers.com's content and provide a mechanism to deploy consistent messaging across all platforms.
  • 1800flowers.com needs a social media strategy to reach users on Instagram, Facebook and other social platforms. New flower startups, such as Bouqs, Farmgirl Flowers, and BloomThat, are proving to be much more successful at reaching large audiences on social media.
  • Corporate social responsibility is important to the millennial generation, 1800flowers.com has several CSR initiatives that are currently buried in the website. Reprioritizing and deploying this information should be part of the content strategy.
  • Finding new and creative ways to reach the millennial market is key. This generation is already influencing bouquet styles, purchasing options, and delivery methods. Flowers are being delivered on bicycles, in burlap sacks and through monthly subscription services.
