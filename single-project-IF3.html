CASE STUDY

PROJECT INFO

Role: UX & UI Designer

Tools: Sketch, Craft & InVision

Date: Nov. 15, 2016

Prototype: https://invis.io/M2EX8PRTX

CHALLENGE

Complete a UX brief and research plan to evaluate 1800flowers.com's competitors and target audience. Use findings to make improvements in navigation, UI elements and overall usability. Success will be measured by implementation of identified solutions on desktop, tablet, and mobile as well as recommendations to increase revenue.

 

SOLUTION

The navigation menu was restructured to avoid unnecessary confusion and simplify usability. A mega menu was implemented to accommodate a large number of options while revealing lower-level site pages at a glance. Steps to get through checkout were minimized to decrease cart abandonment and basket ring. The overall aesthetic and tone of the website was redesigned to appeal to the target audience, the millennial generation.



RESEARCH METHODS & TOOLS

Competitive research, quantitative analysis, user interviews, card sort, personas and journey maps.

KEY INSIGHTS
  • Millennials currently buy more flowers than any other demographic, 66% of buyers are women
  • Competition is increasing as well-funded start-ups actively target the millennial market
  • 1800flowers.com is underperforming in its ability to reach millennial market both in terms of messaging, social media strategy and the overall aesthetic of their website
  • Multiple navigation bars are confusing
  • Interviewees noted buying flowers for birthdays or anniversaries and in some cases as a sign of sympathy.
