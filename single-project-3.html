CHALLENGE

Millennials are one of the largest generations in history and they are moving into their prime spending years. They are forecasted to reshape the economy and force companies to examine how they do business for decades to come. 1800-Flowers is underperforming in its ability to reach this audience. Online flower startups are rapidly emerging to meet the unique needs of this tech-savvy generation. 1800flowers is at risk of losing a valuable opportunity to reach millennial consumers.

 

SOLUTION

Millennials put a premium on speed, efficiency, and convenience in their transactions. Improving the navigation and checkout process across platforms is key to attracting and retaining millennial consumers. Through a heuristic analysis and card sort, the page navigation was restructured to avoid unnecessary confusion and improve usability. A mega menu was implemented to accommodate a large number of options while revealing lower-level site pages at a glance. Steps to get through checkout were minimized to decrease cart abandonment. The overall aesthetic and tone of the website was redesigned to be more appealing to the millennial generation.



RESEARCH

This project included the following methods and tools: competitive research, quantitative analysis, user interviews, card sort, personas, and journey maps.

KEY INSIGHTS
  • Millennials currently buy more flowers than any other demographic, 66% of buyers are women
  • Competition is increasing as well-funded start-ups actively target the millennial market
  • 1800flowers.com is underperforming in its ability to reach millennial market both in terms of messaging, social media strategy and the overall aesthetic of their website
  • Multiple navigation bars are confusing
  • Interviewees noted buying flowers for birthdays or anniversaries and in some cases as a sign of sympathy.
